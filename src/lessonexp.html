Intel Report 1: What are exponents?

In order to understand exponents, you must also understand multiplication. Exponents are basically repetitive multiplication.
The form of an exponent is:
ab,
where a is the base and b is the power or exponent.
The power tells you how many times you should multiply the base.

(Note: Any number with a power 0 is equal to 1. For example, 30 = 1.)

Intel Report 2: Examples

Example 1.
32 = 3 x 3 = 9

Example 2.
43 = 4 x 4 x 4 = 64

Example 3.
52 + 62 = 25 + 36 = 61
