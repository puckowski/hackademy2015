Intel Report 1: What is Modulo Division?

This section is a continuation of the previous Lesson (Division) more specifically division with remainders. This is basically a remainder. You will need to understand multiplication and division. The concept of modulo math is that if there isn’t a remainder then your answer is 0. If the divisor doesn’t go into the dividend evenly then the remainder is your answer.
(Note: The picture below should help with understanding modulo division.)


Below are some examples:
Example 1.
12 ÷ 4 = 3, which means the remainder is 0,
therefore, 12 mod 4 = 0.
12 = 4 x 3 + 0.

Example 2.
11 ÷ 2 = 4 with a remainder of 3,
therefore, 11 mod 2 = 3.
11 = 2 x 4 + 3

Note: Another symbol for mod is the percent sign %.
Therefore, 12 mod 5 and 12 % 5 mean the same thing.
