Intel Report 1: What is Multiplication?

Multiplication is basically addition but more simplified. In multiplication, the order of the numbers do not matter.

Intel Report 2: The Times Table and Examples

Memorizing the times table shown below can be helpful when learning multiplication.
To use the table, you take a number of the outside row and and column and after you "multiply" them together, where they meet on the table is the answer.

Example 1.
5 x 3 = 15 is equivalent to 3 x 5 = 15
Similarly,
3 x 5 is the same as 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 and
5 x 3 is the same as 5 + 5 + 5

Example 2.
10 x 5 = 50 is the same as
10 + 10 + 10 + 10 + 10.

You can also multiply negative numbers together as well:
If you multiply a negative number by a positive number, the product will be negative.
If you multiply a negative number by a negative number, the product is positive.

Example 3.
12 x -4 = -48
-12 x -4 = 48
