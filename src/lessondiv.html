Intel Report 1: What is Division?

Division is the opposite of multiplication. If you understand multiplication, divison will come easily. For example, the number 20 can be divided or split into many ways. The main format of a division problem is shown below:
dividend ÷ divisor = quotient

Example 1.
20 ÷ 5 = 4
20 is the dividend
5 is the divisor
4 is the quotient.

Just think of division as "how many times a number (divisor) fit into another number (dividend)."

Example 2.
20 can be divided by 2, 4, 5, 10, and 20.
20 ÷ 2 = 10
20 ÷ 4 = 5
20 ÷ 5 = 4
20 ÷ 10 = 2
20 ÷ 20 = 1

Intel Report 2: Division with Remainders

Although there are numbers that divide nicely, there are also numbers that do not get divided evenly. These numbers are left with what is called a remainder. See the example below to see how remainders work.

Example 3.
20 ÷ 3 = 6 with a remainder of 2

In this example, 3 (the divisor) only goes in evenly to 20 (the dividend) 6 times. After that you only have 2 left to reach 20, so the remainder for this problem is 2.

Example 4.

50 ÷ 12 = 4 with a remainder of 2
This is saying that 12 can only go into 50 four times, and on the fifth time there is only 2 remaining. Therefore, the remainder is 2.
